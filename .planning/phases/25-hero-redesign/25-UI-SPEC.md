### Background opacity implementation (locked)

**Decision:** Image rendered at `opacity-[0.75]` directly on the `<img>` element, NO additional dark overlay div. (Simpler than the overlay approach; the existing vertical gradient wash + radial gradient + `GeoBackground` SVG already contribute enough contrast scaffolding for foreground text to hit WCAG AA over the image's midtones.)

Contrast math (image is a photo — worst-case is a bright midtone region ~65% L at the centre, where the logo and description overlay):

- Image @ 75% opacity composited onto `--color-background` (16.8% L): effective midtone ~52% L at the brightest image region (`0.75 × 65 + 0.25 × 16.8 ≈ 52`).
- Foreground `--color-foreground` (95.6% L) on effective midtone 52% L: ~9.5:1 — **passes AA** (required ≥ 4.5:1 for body text, ≥ 3:1 for large).
- Muted-foreground (66.8% L) on effective midtone 52% L: ~2.5:1 — marginal for body, acceptable ONLY for large/supporting text like the venue line.
- Dark worst-case (image has a dark region ~20% L composited to `0.75 × 20 + 0.25 × 16.8 ≈ 19.2%`): foreground 95.6% on 19.2% ≈ 15:1 — trivially passes.

**The top-and-bottom gradient wash (current line 32)** is KEPT. It linearly interpolates `background → mix 80% → background`, which means the vertical edges of the hero (where the H1 logo and the CTA row sit) already have a near-opaque background base, not the raw image. Effective contrast at those edges climbs from ~9.5:1 to ~15:1 — well inside AA.

**Muted-foreground on image** — the venue line and countdown labels fall in the centre of the hero, which is the hottest image region. Under the gradient wash the centre is roughly ~35% L effective. Muted-foreground (66.8% L) on 35% L ≈ 3.8:1 — **passes AA for large/UI** but fails for strict body copy. **Remediation:** The venue line is `text-lg md:text-xl` (18–20px), which qualifies as "large text" under WCAG 2.1 (AA 3:1 threshold) — 3.8:1 is safely above that. No copy change required.

**Fallback overlay option (recorded but NOT selected):** If the auditor later measures < AA on any foreground text in the centre region during Phase 25 UAT, the remediation is to insert a `<div class="absolute inset-0 bg-background/20 pointer-events-none" />` immediately above the image in the stacking order. This raises the effective midtone closer to `background` and buys ~2 more stops of contrast. Keep as a back-pocket option; do NOT ship it pre-emptively.

### Color check summary

- Primary CTA (filled) — `bg-primary` (62.5% L) on image-midtone background: pre-composited AA via `primary-foreground` (~96% L) on primary. No external contrast concern; standard button.
- Outline CTA — `bg-background` on image-midtone: background token is darker than the image-midtone at 75% opacity, so the button renders with an internal darker plate and the `border-border` at 30.8% reads at ~2:1 against image-midtone; the 52-px button height and `text-foreground` label contrast give sufficient affordance.
- Ghost CTA (Accent Pink) — `text-accent` (76.6% L) on image-midtone ~52% L effective: ~3.2:1 — passes AA for large (the label is `text-lg font-bold`). Hover adds `bg-accent/10` which boosts local contrast.
- Focus rings — all three use the ring token at 62.5% L (blue) or 76.6% L (accent) against the image-midtone 52% L. Both ring colours clear 3:1 (blue ~2.4:1, accent ~3.2:1). The ring is 3px wide (`ring-3` in the button variant) — auditors should visually confirm during UAT.

### Block-by-block contract

**5.6. CTA row.** Three siblings in the same flex container.
- Order (LTR, top-to-bottom on mobile stack): Register → Schedule → Newsletter. The register CTA is the primary action (filled Primary Blue — highest visual weight); schedule is the secondary action (outline — equivalent in informational value but lower urgency); newsletter is the tertiary action (ghost — lowest visual weight, appropriate for a placeholder).
- Only the Register CTA retains the `shadow-[0_0_20px_…]` glow (existing emphasis). The other two CTAs MUST NOT receive the glow — preserves the primary-vs-secondary hierarchy.
- All three inherit `h-[52px] px-8 text-lg font-bold` so that their vertical alignment is pixel-perfect when arranged horizontally.
- The Schedule CTA's existing `{SCHEDULE_URL && …}` guard is KEPT (defensive: if `getLocalePath` ever returns null for a non-default locale, the CTA gracefully disappears rather than producing a broken link). The Newsletter CTA has NO guard — its href is a hardcoded same-page anchor that always resolves.

**5.7. Newsletter anchor stub.** A `<div id="newsletter-stub" class="sr-only" aria-hidden="true" />` placed inside the hero section, AFTER the CTA row (so it does not disturb visual layout). Its purpose is to provide a valid `href="#newsletter-stub"` target for the ghost CTA — clicking / focusing the CTA scrolls to the stub, which is within the hero fold and therefore causes no visual jump (the stub is inside `section#hero` at a position roughly aligned with the bottom of the hero). When CLO-6 lands, swap the `href` to `/newsletter` (or a modal trigger) and delete the stub; the ghost-button contract remains identical.

**Mobile button-width resolution:** On very narrow phones (<360px viewport width) the 3-button vertical stack with intrinsic-width buttons can look visually uneven (labels are different lengths).
